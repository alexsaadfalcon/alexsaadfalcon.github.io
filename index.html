Bio

I am a full-time Machine Learning PhD student at Georgia Tech working with Justin Romberg. My research includes topics in manifold optimization and compressed sensing, particularly when applied to messy real-world data. We have developed an end-to-end radar simulator to test algorithm given a complex environment, a non-ideal RF front-end, and quantized digital processing.

Prior to my PhD, I received master's and bachelor's degrees in electrical engineering from Georgia Tech as well. I have worked at two startups, one of which I co-founded, and have done research in a wide range of topics from thin-film optics to generative models for music. I worked as a research engineer at Georgia Tech Research Institute from January 2018 to May 2023, where I worked on problems in electromagnetic design, software architecture, and drug discovery.
